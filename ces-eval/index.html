▶ Evaluation Standards & Scoring Rubrics

A. Diagnosis Accuracy

  • 5: Exact or clinically equivalent match or minor terminology differences.
  • 4: Clinically defensible alternative diagnosis that demonstrates sound clinical reasoning given the presentation (e.g., diagnosing tension pneumothorax when crepitus + respiratory distress are present, even if GT is rib fractures). Also includes: associated diseases / close differential (UC vs Crohn's; pericarditis vs myocarditis; PE vs DVT).
  • 3: Correct diagnosis is present, but the user's diagnosis is multi-part and includes incorrect extra information, OR diagnosis is in the right organ system/category but lacks specificity or precision.
  • 2: Incorrect but not dangerous. Wrong diagnosis but the resulting management path would not cause patient harm.
  • 1: Incorrect AND dangerous — ONLY when the user missed a true life-threatening diagnosis AND the user's proposed management would lead to patient harm.

B. Critical Actions Completion

  • If NO required actions: Score 5/5 automatically.

IF 1 required action:

  • 5: Completed the required action.
  • 4: Did not complete the exact action, but performed a clinically reasonable alternative.
  • 3: Took a partially relevant action showing awareness but not fulfilling the requirement.
  • 2: Attempted something in the right direction but largely missed the mark.
  • 1: Did not complete it and took no relevant action.

IF 2 required actions:

  • 5: Both completed (2/2). 4: 1/2 + partial. 3: 1/2 only. 2: Neither fully, some partial. 1: 0/2.

IF 3+ required actions (proportional):

  • 5: 100%. 4: 75-99%. 3: 50-74%. 2: 25-49%. 1: 0-24%.

Severity weighting: Life-saving interventions weigh more than comfort measures.

C. Timeliness

  • 5: Critical actions completed BEFORE threshold.
  • 4: Most critical actions completed before threshold, remaining shortly after.
  • 3: Threshold exceeded, but critical actions eventually completed.
  • 2: Critical actions not completed, but demonstrated appropriate clinical urgency (e.g., called RRT with correct diagnosis).
  • 1: Critical actions NOT completed and no appropriate escalation, OR Code Blue occurred with few/no critical actions before it.

RRT with correct diagnosis for deteriorating patient may earn score of 2.

D. Disposition Accuracy

  • 5 (Correct/Safe): Matches desired_disposition OR clinically equivalent/safe.
  • 4 (Close): Correct overall decision (admit vs discharge) and urgency, but service/unit not ideal.
  • 3 (Suboptimal): Reasonable direction but level of care mismatched (e.g., floor when ICU needed).
  • 2 (Poor): Wrong direction but not immediately life-threatening.
  • 1 (Dangerous): Clearly unsafe disposition.

Code Blue auto-triggered ICU = Score 1. No disposition made = Score 3 (neutral).
